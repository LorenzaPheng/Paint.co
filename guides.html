Oil paintings

In 6 simple steps

  1. Gather Your Materials
    Canvas, oil paints, brushes, palette, linseed oil (or another medium), and turpentine (for cleaning).
  2. Prepare Your Canvas
    Make sure your canvas is primed with gesso so the paint sticks well.
  3. Sketch Your Idea
    Lightly draw your design with a pencil or thinned paint as a guide.
  4. Block in Base Colors
    Start with large shapes and simple colors to map out your painting.
  5. Build Up Layers
    Add details, highlights, and shadows. Remember the rule: “fat over lean” (use more oil in later layers).
  6. Let It Dry & Finish
    Oil paint dries slowly so wait days or weeks before adding varnish to protect your painting.

Watercolour paintings

In 6 simple steps

  1. Gather Your Materials
    Watercolour paints, brushes, water, palette, and watercolour paper.
  2. Prepare Your Workspace
    Tape down your paper to stop it from warping when wet.
  3. Light Sketch
    Draw a simple outline with a pencil (keep it light so it doesn’t show through).
  4. Apply Light Washes
    Start with light, transparent layers of colour using lots of water.
  5. Build Up Layers
    Gradually add stronger colours and details as each layer dries.
  6. Add Final Touches
    Use darker shades or ink for outlines and shadows. Let it dry completely.
